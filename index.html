Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Borlaug was born in 1914 in Cresco, Iowa. He studied plant pathology at the University of Minnesota and then went on to work for the Rockefeller Foundation. In the 1940s, he began working on developing high-yielding varieties of wheat that could be grown in developing countries. His work was successful, and by the 1960s, he had helped to increase wheat production in developing countries by over 100%.

Dr. Borlaug was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his work on the Green Revolution. He died in 2009 at the age of 95.
